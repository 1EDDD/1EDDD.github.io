Executive Summary

This study documents the evolution of Apple's software throttling mechanisms, specifically on "Legacy Architecture" devices (e.g., A13 Bionic). By manipulating key flags in MobileGestalt.plist, we isolated the system's thermal control logic. Data from the latest iOS 26.1 environment reveals a strategic shift: the OS now allows unrestricted single-core speeds but enforces aggressive caps on Multi-Core parallelism and Neural Engine tasks to extend battery life on aging hardware, sacrificing nearly 170% of potential AI performance.

1.0 Phase 1: The Baseline (iOS 18 Era)

In initial experiments, disabling compression keys resulted in a generalized performance uplift. Throttling was applied uniformly across the CPU, acting as a broad dampener to minimize power draw.

2.0 Phase 2: Targeted Suppression (iOS 26.1)

Tests conducted on the latest iOS 26.1 builds indicate a more sophisticated "Selective Throttling" engine.

2.1 The "Parallelism Cap" Strategy

The system now recognizes simple interactions and boosts Single-Core performance to max potential (to maintain a smooth UI feel). However, it brutally throttles heavy Multi-Core loads and computational photography tasks to protect older batteries.

Geekbench 6 Metric Stock Configuration Modified Configuration Delta
CPU Single-Core 1,776 1,774 0% (Optimized)
CPU Multi-Core 3,083 3,981 +29.1% (Suppressed)
Object Detection (AI) 915 img/sec 2,533 img/sec +176.8%

Critical Analysis: The dramatic surge in Object Detection (+176%) proves that the legacy Neural Engine is capable of far higher throughput. The bottleneck is not the hardware's age, but a deliberate software limit likely imposed to mitigate thermal aging.

3.1 Conclusion: Planned Obsolescence vs. Protection

The research concludes that the perceived slowdown of legacy iPhones on newer firmware is partially a software choice. While thermalmonitord aims to protect aging components, the applied limits are excessively conservative.

By overriding these "Safety Wheels," users can reclaim up to 30% Multi-Core speed and nearly double the AI processing power, effectively restoring the device's original computational relevancy.
